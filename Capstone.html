About the dataset¶

In [2]:
df = pd.read_csv('CompleteDataset.csv', low_memory=False)

The Fifa 18 Complete Player Dataset features in-game attributes of all the players in the video game Fifa 18 by EA. The data is scraped from the website https://sofifa.com by extracting the Player personal data and Player Ids and then the playing and style statistics. Each attribute has been assigned by EA based off of each players performances from previous years. This dataset contains approximately 18,000 players with 75 attributes each.

Acknowledgements¶

This dataset was provided by Aman Shrivastava on kaggle.com.

Summary¶

It is generally accepted that a young player has a lot of potential and as he gets older he becomes a better soccer player. After a few golden years at his peak performance, that player eventually gets older and his skills deteriorate. I want to know (on average) which ages are the "golden years" and how much of an impact age has when it comes to EA's rankings for player's potential. Once the 'Golden years' have been established, I want to compare the average wage by age to the Golden years and see if there is a connection.

Also, while we are taking a look at age and potential. I want to explore the average age and the average potential of each nation since these two factors may have an impact of future performances for international teams. I will also be paying close attention to the number of players each nation has since some nations may not have a large enough sample group to be useful and there may be a corellation between the number of players and a countries average potential.

Hypothesis¶

I predict that the relationship between age and overall score will be a natural bell curve with players overall rankings being low at younger ages and peaking around 25 years of age then beginning to slope back down. I assume age is a direct factor in EA's potential rankings so I expect that the average potential for each age group will be highest at the youngest ages and steadily decrease with age. I predict the average wage a player makes based on age will be very similar to the relationship between age and overall score.

If soccer has been a part of a countries culture for a long time then more people will be playing it. If more people are playing the sport then more people will be playing professional from that country around the world. As the sport naturally progresses it becomes more difficult for players to become a professional, therefor increasing the quality of the players from the country. I believe EA keeps this in mind when assigning potential rankings so I predict that the nations with the most amount of players will have the highest potential even if their average age may be lower than others.

The "Wage" and "Value" columns are currently a string formatted as a money value. Let's make a function that will reformat these values as a float.

In [5]:
def extract_value_from(value):
    out = value.replace('', '')
    if 'M' in out:
        out = float(out.replace('M', ''))*1000000
    elif 'K' in value:
        out = float(out.replace('K', ''))*1000
    return float(out)

df['Wage'] = df['Wage'].apply(lambda x: extract_value_from(x))
df['Wage'] = df['Wage'].apply(lambda x: x/1000)
df['Value'] = df['Value'].apply(lambda x: extract_value_from(x))
df['Value'] = df['Value'].apply(lambda x: x/1000000)
